# Bear Hunt (Bear Trap) Guide — Whiteout Survival

## What Bear Hunt is (and why it matters)
Bear Hunt is an alliance damage event where your alliance summons a **Raging Bear** and everyone attacks it for **30 minutes**. Rewards are primarily tied to **how much damage you personally contribute** (and the alliance’s overall performance). :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

The key mindset: this is a **DPS race**, not a survival fight. You’re trying to squeeze as many high-damage rally hits as possible into a short window. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

---

## Requirements, timing, and basic mechanics
- The hunt is triggered by alliance leadership (typically R4/R5) after your alliance meets the requirement to build the hunting trap. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
- Duration: **30 minutes** from the moment it starts. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
- Cooldown: alliances can summon on a recurring cooldown (commonly described as about every ~2 days / ~47–48 hours depending on the system). :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
- There have been game updates that changed structure (including the introduction of a second Bear Trap for time zones and changes to reward emphasis). Your alliance should align rules to the current version you’re playing. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

---

## The “real” objective: maximize hits per minute
Most alliances lose damage for one simple reason: **downtime**.
Downtime happens when:
- rallies aren’t being launched continuously
- marches take too long to reach the trap
- too few rally leaders are calling
- rallies fill too slowly, or too fast (locking people out)

Your goal is to keep a **steady pipeline** of rallies so that nearly everyone is always:
1) marching to a rally, 2) hitting the bear, or 3) returning and rejoining.

## Alliance setup (leaders)
### 1) Place the Hunting Trap smartly
A common best practice is to keep the trap inside alliance territory and positioned so members can reach it quickly. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

### 2) Rally leader positioning is a big deal
Rally leaders should **teleport close to the trap** before the event starts. Shorter march time = more rallies launched within 30 minutes. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

If your alliance is serious about results, make this a rule:
- “If you are a rally leader, you must be close to the trap before start.”

### 3) Pre-assign rally leaders and use waves
A proven approach is to assign rally leaders into **waves** so the alliance doesn’t launch everything at once and then stall:
- Wave 1: 6–8 leaders call immediately
- Wave 2: 6–8 leaders call ~1.5–2 minutes later
- Wave 3 (optional): another wave if rallies are filling too fast and people can’t join :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

This keeps rallies available throughout the 30 minutes and prevents “everyone spams rallies, then nobody has rallies to join.”

### 4) One job: keep rallies available
Leaders should be focused on two things:
- keep launching rallies
- keep rally timers tight (don’t set long rally timers if it slows down the cycle)

## Player preparation (everyone)
### Checklist before Bear Hunt starts
- Be online 5–10 minutes early
- Queue up your best march presets (so you can rejoin fast)
- Make sure your troops are not stuck in other activities (gathering, long marches, etc.)
- If your alliance uses “join rules” (like minimum troops), know them before it begins

### Save a Bear preset
Many players improve immediately just by saving a dedicated “Bear” preset of heroes + troops so joining is instant. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

## Heroes: what matters and what doesn’t
Bear Hunt runs as an expedition-style combat mode where your heroes’ relevant skills matter for output. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

General guidance that holds up across alliances:
- Rally leaders should use their strongest **damage-focused expedition hero lineup**
- Joiners should also prioritize damage-focused heroes; don’t bring “tanky” logic into a DPS event :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Important rally mechanic that many people miss:
- In expedition rally content, early joiners can heavily influence the rally’s effective buffs/skills (commonly explained as the first few joiners matter most). This is why alliances often want their strongest damage joiners to be among the earliest in rallies. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Practical rule:
- If your alliance is pushing damage records, coordinate who joins first (strong damage accounts first, then everyone else).

## Troops and formations (DPS over durability)
Because the event is about damage, alliances often lean into **high damage troop mixes** (commonly emphasizing marksmen more than infantry). :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

The exact “best” ratio varies by your tech, heroes, and your alliance rules, but the consistent theme is:
- don’t over-invest in low-DPS “just to survive”
- optimize for damage output and rally throughput :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}

If your alliance already has a standard ratio (example: “10/10/80” style rules), follow alliance rules first—consistency beats personal experiments when coordinating at scale. :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}

## How to participate efficiently (joiner playbook)
1) Open Bear Hunt as soon as it starts.
2) Join rallies immediately—don’t wait for “the perfect” rally.
3) As soon as a march returns, rejoin again.
4) Avoid slow actions mid-event (gear swapping, long menu browsing, etc.). Do that before start.

Key efficiency tip:
- If you’re far from the trap, it can be better to **join rallies called by leaders near the trap** rather than calling your own far-away rallies (longer travel time wastes total alliance hits). :contentReference[oaicite:16]{index=16}

## How to run Bear Hunt like a pro alliance (leader rules)
These are optional rules that drastically improve results:

### Rule A: “Only assigned callers start rallies”
This prevents rally spam that causes half-filled rallies and chaos.
Use waves so there’s always something to join. :contentReference[oaicite:17]{index=17}

### Rule B: “Teleport requirements for callers”
Callers must be near trap before start. :contentReference[oaicite:18]{index=18}

### Rule C: “No idle marches”
If you’re online during Bear Hunt, your marches should be participating unless your role is specifically “fill last” or “support.”

### Rule D: “Strong joiners join early”
Because early joiners can affect the rally’s overall buff/skill effectiveness, your strongest damage players should be trained to join quickly at rally launch. :contentReference[oaicite:19]{index=19}

## Common mistakes (and how to fix them)
1) **Too many rally leaders**
- Result: half-full rallies, wasted time
- Fix: assign callers and run waves :contentReference[oaicite:20]{index=20}

2) **Callers are far from the trap**
- Result: long march time, fewer rallies launched
- Fix: teleport callers close before start :contentReference[oaicite:21]{index=21}

3) **People wait instead of joining**
- Result: downtime and fewer hits
- Fix: “join anything” rule unless the rally is clearly low quality

4) **No preset / slow joining**
- Result: you lose multiple rally cycles
- Fix: save a dedicated bear preset :contentReference[oaicite:22]{index=22}

5) **Uncoordinated first joiners**
- Result: rallies don’t get the best early-buff contribution
- Fix: assign 2–4 fast strong joiners as “first in” :contentReference[oaicite:23]{index=23}

## Quick FAQ
### Do I need to heal after Bear Hunt?
Most community guides describe Bear Hunt as being different from PvP/heavy loss modes, and many players report not needing the same kind of healing cycle as war events. Still, game versions and specifics can vary—watch your post-fight troop state and follow your alliance’s local rule. :contentReference[oaicite:24]{index=24}

### Should I solo attack?
Most alliances discourage soloing because coordinated rallies drive better total throughput and keep more people participating. If your alliance has a rule, follow it.

### My damage is low—what’s the fastest improvement?
- Join more rallies (volume beats perfect theory)
- Use a saved bear preset
- Join quickly when rallies start (especially if you’re a strong account) :contentReference[oaicite:25]{index=25}
